  • For each 1ml of fill, the sachets internal area must be at least 8cm²
  • The sachet is to be free from excessive air
  • Sachets must withstand at least 1,500kg (3,300lb) pressure for 10 seconds
  • As a fluage test, bonding must resist 5 hours under 1,000kg

Laminate composition

  • 12 micron PET (Polyester)
  • 12 micron Aluminium (Foil)
  • 75 micron PE (Polyethylene)
  • The polyethylene must be modified linear low (LLPE) or equivalent
  • The overall dimensions must remain the same throughout the production run

Minimum overall size

  • 85mm (h) x 85mm (w)

Optimum size

  • 160mm (h) x 140mm (w)

Maximum total weight

  • Including fill: 20 grams
  • The contents must be accurately regulated and controlled so it remains the same measurement throughout the production run

Approval of trial samples

For initial approval to tip-on or onsert into Australian Doctor, we require a minimum of no less than 200 samples, together with a written declaration that the production run complies with our specifications and will match the samples provided in every detail.

Once initially approved and the full production run produced, we will require an additional quantity for approval.

This quantity must be drawn at regular intervals to represent the entire production run. We require one sachet for every 650 sachets to be produced from the full print run, or a minimum of 250 sachets.

Due to the mechanics of tipping-on inserts, there may be occasions where a very small quantity of magazines are bound without the insert. As such, it would be advisable not to run wording offering a sachet within the magazine.

Throughout the binding run, samples will be taken every 15 minutes. At the end of each shift the collected samples will be pressure tested and checked thoroughly.

Client agreement

During binding, should Reed Business Information (RBI) believe there is a potential problem with the sachets which could could cause physical damage to the publication - RBI reserves the right to suspend inserting without any compensation to, or ramification from the client.

RBI requires the client to sign a contract guaranteeing payment of any loss of revenue suffered by RBI and indemnifying us from any claims from them in the event of a problem with a client-supplied ‘sachet’.
