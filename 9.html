Advertorial Guidelines

Details

Advertorials are generally considered to be appropriate when advertisers have a complicated message to convey to their customers, or a particular product promotion warrants a more sophisticated or educational approach.

Used as a back-up to existing advertising, advertorials can add a new dimension to a promotional campaign and help create audience awareness. Advertorials generally follow a compatible style and design to the magazine to ensure both readers and advertisers benefit from the look and familiar reading style of the publication.

Requirements

Listed below are the generally accepted guidelines.

  • All advertorials must have “ADVERTISEMENT” or “ADVERTORIAL” clearly printed on the top of each page in not less than 8 point (A4 advert or smaller) or 12 point (A3 full page).
  • The name of the magazine must not be used, to infer that the advertorial has been written by the publication’s editorial team.
  • All advertorials must be distinct from the style of the magazine and not use Sabon or Helvetica Neue as fonts or the same column format.
  • Australian Doctor colours must not be used.
  • The editor reserves the right to reject an advertorial which does not comply with these guidelines.
  • We do not advise more than three pictures per page.

Wording for ads which don't have product names

In the case of advertisements which do not show the name of the product, they should have either the word "Advertisement" (8 point - A4 advert or smaller or 12 point - A3 full page) or the company logo somewhere within the artwork to signify to readers that it is an advertisement rather than editorial.
